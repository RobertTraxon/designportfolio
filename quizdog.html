Problem

Design a portal for educators to assign tasks, track results, and communicate with children with a multi-layer subscription-based model.

Research

I put out a survey to find out how teachers used educational platforms to help identify which areas were priorities. Here are some of the key questions I considered when gathering data for this project:

  • How often do you use digital educational platforms in your teaching?
  • What types of digital educational platforms have you used?
  • What features are most important to you?
  • What challenges have you encountered while using digital educational platforms?

Most use digital platforms to assign and grade homework, conduct assessments, and provide additional resources to students.
Teachers use a mixture of learning management systems, educational apps, and video conferencing tools.
Self-marking ability and assigning or tracking homework are the most important.
Technical difficulties and student engagement issues are the most common issues.
User Needs
Engagement
From the student's perspective, the platform needs to be engaging to encourage completion of set tasks.
Time saving
For the teacher or parent, saving time by marking and getting accurate feedback is a priority.
Streamline
Streamlining multiple products or services into a single offering would also reduce the complexity of navigating between apps.
Business Needs
Retention
Working as a subscription-based model, retaining customers is the most important factor.
Premium
Convert as many free users as possible to its premium subscription model in order to remove reliance on advertisements.
Solution

Design an educational platform to help streamline digital assessment and provide students with an engaging and reliable experience.

Integrated Platform

With the survey results showing a real mix of tools used within education, I wanted to combine a few of the most crucial into the platform. The primary focus is on making it as easy as possible to keep track of tasks and results and plan lessons around them accordingly.

Task Management

To ensure the lessons or tasks created are simple to keep track of, I wanted to take inspiration from systems like Jira to provide an identifiable layout to create, assign, and track tasks given to students.

Data

A focus on self-marking and providing teachers with data on their pupils' performance would provide the key USP for upgrading to a premium account.

Premium

As the model is reliant on encouraging premium and school-level memberships over the basic free option, an emphasis on keeping hidden content within the app design will encourage users to upgrade out of fear of missing out.
