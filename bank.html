Problem

The challenge for this project was to design a new banking app that would provide support for the Northbank customer base.

Research

As I designed this product from scratch, the research was integral to seeing where users typically experience pain points when banking online. It was vital to understand the primary purpose for which users log in and reflect that in the user journey. Here were some of the key questions I considered when gathering data for this project:

  1. What is the target demographic?
  2. What are the primary concerns when banking online?
  3. What are the main purposes for using banking apps?
  4. How often do people visit banking apps?

Over 74% of the UK uses mobile banking, with increasing numbers of senior and millennial users switching to mobile apps each year.
Fraud and security are the primary concerns for those banking online, with 47% of respondents seeing the importance of card turn-off option.
90% of users visit banking apps to view their balance, with 79% also viewing recent transactions.
The most common frequency for visiting a banking app is at least once a week.
Interest-based loans typically account for 65% of a bank's income.
User Needs
Finances
The primary reason most users access banking apps is to see a comprehensive view of their finances.
Security
Whether it's reporting a stolen card or protecting money online, security is another major concern for users.
Support
A criticism of online banking is that customers often feel less connected with their bank, so offering a good level of support is key.
Business Needs
Assistance
A well-developed banking app can save a business from having to hire extra staff to support answering customer queries.
Products
A bank makes most of its money through the interest on products such as mortgages, loans, and credit cards. It is therefore important to try to upsell these products where possible.
Solution

Design a banking app that highlights the main areas users visit when banking online.

3 Click Rule

I wanted to create a portal that offered all the vital elements of banking in as few clicks as possible. With viewing balances and outgoings being the primary focus for logging on, the landing page would have to reflect this. It would also be beneficial to provide support and security assistance within good reach to help minimise the need for users to contact the bank directly if it can be avoided.

Prototyping
Information Architecture

By displaying all accounts in a scrollable container, it maintains a more fluid feel when navigating between the transactions for different products. This made it quicker for users to distinguish between their finances and place a clearer emphasis on their most active accounts.

Security & Support

Another major concern for users is security and support. Instead of hiding these away behind a more complex user-journey, I wanted them to be accessible from any point in the app within less than 3 clicks. Placing these in the main account navigation menu was a great way to keep them within reach and provide comfort for users who require assistance.
