Problem +

Whether it’s a suggestion from a user or speculative analysis within the business, each project starts with a problem or gap in the market.

Research +

Each idea is then investigated using a combination of appropriate qualitative or quantitative research. This is to ensure each idea is supported by the best data available and to help me pinpoint key areas for improvement.

User Needs +

The next step is being able to prioritise what the user needs from the project and being able to distinguish it from what the user wants.

Business Needs +

It is also important to understand the goals, desires and limitations of the business itself and grade any ideas to assess the impact and effort of the project.

Solution +

With all the research and data in place, it's now time to form a proper solution to the problem. Whether it's personas, user-flow or an information architecture diagram, It’s important to create a clear response, highlighting the proposed issues and how I plan to address them.

Prototype +

Now for the fun part! Creating low and high fidelity wireframes and using a range of prototyping tools to bring the solution to life.

Development +

Once the prototypes have been thoroughly tested and approved its now time to move forward to the development stage. Whether this is passed onto another team or developed myself, it's important to ensure the same standard of quality is maintained from concept through to completion.
